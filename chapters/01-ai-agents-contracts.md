The best operators I know treat AI agents like contracts.

Not employees. Not friends. Contracts.

You define the scope. They execute within it. No ambiguity, no feelings, no "I thought you meant..."

The moment you start anthropomorphizing them, you start overtrusting them.

**Contracts don't understand intent. Neither do LLMs.**

This isn't cynicism. It's clarity.

When you negotiate with a contractor, you write tight specs. You don't assume they "get" the bigger picture. You check deliverables. You define success before work starts.

Same with agents.

> "The moment you start anthropomorphizing them, you start overtrusting them."

If you've ever been frustrated by an AI "not listening" — you probably anthropomorphized it first.
